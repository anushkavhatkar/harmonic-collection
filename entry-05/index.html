entry 05: developments

Until the mid-1800s, all dyes came from natural sources, such as insects, roots, or minerals. Producing them was difficult and expensive.

Sea snails like these are found around the world. When poked (milked) or crushed, they secrete a substance that was used for making purple dye as early as 1570 BC. Thousands of snails were needed to color a single robe, and for centuries purple dye was more valuable than silver and worn only by royalty.
